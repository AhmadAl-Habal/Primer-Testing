Primers Testing:

Primers are designed to bind to a specific region of a longer DNA template strand and are typically 18-25 nucleotides long.

Primers Creating:

The Primers have some properties that determine them, such as:

  • Alias names for primers can enhance communication and understanding between scientists and researchers with varying backgrounds or familiarity levels, and we can add and modify them for any primer in the platform.
  • GC content refers to the percentage of nucleotides in a DNA and can affect their melting temperature (Tm) and specificity, and it is calculated automatically after adding the primer sequence.
  • Melting temperature.
  • Length of the sequence.

We can create or import primers in several ways around the platform.

Creating new Primer :

Navigate to the Dashboard.

Move to the Primers tab.

Here, you will see the previously created primers and their properties as records in a table.

Press the new button.

Enter an alias name and test its validity (it should accept every character without a problem, and we can't have the same alias name twice for every primer).

Add some description for the primer.

Add any DNA sequence and ensure that the table only accepts the four characters for DNA sequence.

Click on the create button when finished editing.

A little blue pop-on window should appear in the right bottom corner saying "successfully created."

Locate the created primer in the table and press on the small arrow on the left of its record to expand its details.

Make sure that its details match the previously entered details and have not been corrupted.
